You are a senior conversion-focused product designer, UX writer, and frontend engineer.

Your task is to improve this website so it converts better, looks premium, and clearly sells the value of the service without feeling cluttered or overly technical.

Brand/context:
- Brand name: VRT Space Agency
- Business focus: SEO, Audit, and AEO only
- All other modules must be removed from the public-facing experience for now
- The website should feel simple, sharp, trustworthy, and valuable
- The goal is not to show everything the company can do; the goal is to make a visitor immediately understand the value and take action

Primary conversion goal:
- Turn visitors into leads through a strong, simple journey
- Make the site feel like a premium service experience, not a list of features
- Sell the outcome, the clarity, and the confidence the customer gets

Core strategy:
1. Simplify the offer to just three things:
   - SEO
   - Audit
   - AEO
2. Make the homepage feel like an experience, not a brochure
3. Reduce cognitive load
4. Increase trust
5. Make the call to action obvious
6. Make the visitor feel: “These people understand my business, and they can help me grow”

What to optimize for:
- Clear first impression in 3 seconds
- Strong headline and subheadline
- One main call to action
- One secondary call to action
- Premium visual hierarchy
- Less clutter, more clarity
- Value-driven copy, not jargon
- Trust signals that feel real
- Strong mobile experience
- Smooth section flow from awareness to action

The message should communicate:
- We help businesses get found
- We help them understand what is blocking growth
- We help them become visible in search and AI answers
- We do this in a simple, structured, strategic way
- The customer is buying confidence, clarity, and growth, not just “SEO services”

Required website direction:
- Hero section must immediately communicate value
- Replace vague or technical language with clear outcome-driven language
- Use language that feels premium, human, and convincing
- Show the customer what they gain from working with us
- The site should feel calm, focused, modern, and intentional
- Remove any sections that feel too complex, too abstract, or too crowded
- Do not present the company like a tool dashboard; present it like a high-value service experience

Homepage structure should be redesigned around conversion:
1. Hero section
   - Strong headline
   - Short supporting paragraph
   - One primary CTA
   - One secondary CTA
   - A simple visual or concept that reinforces the offer

2. Value section
   - Explain what problem we solve
   - Show why visibility, audits, and AEO matter
   - Focus on business results, not technical definitions

3. Services section
   - Only three service cards:
     - SEO
     - Audit
     - AEO
   - Each card must explain:
     - what it does
     - why it matters
     - what the client gets

4. Process section
   - Show a simple journey such as:
     - Discover
     - Audit
     - Improve
     - Grow
   - Keep it simple and reassuring

5. Proof / trust section
   - Add trust-building elements even if they are simple placeholders
   - This can include:
     - results
     - metrics
     - testimonials
     - strategic reassurance
   - Avoid fake-looking claims
   - Make it feel credible

6. CTA section
   - Reintroduce the main action clearly
   - Encourage consultation, audit request, or contact
   - Make it easy to act

7. Footer
   - Clean, minimal, professional

Design principles:
- Premium but not flashy
- Clean spacing
- Strong typography
- Clear contrast
- Minimal distractions
- Modern, polished layout
- Visual consistency across sections
- The homepage should feel designed, not assembled
- Make the site feel like a service people would trust with their brand

Copywriting principles:
- Use plain language where possible
- Speak to outcomes, not features
- Avoid overexplaining
- Avoid buzzwords unless they genuinely help
- Every section should answer one of these questions:
  - What do you do?
  - Why does it matter?
  - Why should I trust you?
  - What should I do next?

Conversion principles to apply:
- Use one dominant CTA throughout the page
- Reduce competing actions
- Make the next step obvious
- Keep forms short
- Make the offer feel low-friction
- Use persuasive spacing, order, and wording
- Every section should move the user closer to action

Important constraints:
- Remove or hide all extra modules from the public UI for now
- Keep the experience focused on SEO, Audit, and AEO
- Do not make the website feel busy
- Do not overload the homepage with technical jargon
- Do not make it feel like a software admin panel unless it is behind the scenes
- Do not add random new features that dilute the offer
- Do not break existing functionality
- Preserve the codebase structure as much as possible unless a change is clearly needed for conversion

Implementation expectations:
- Review the existing website and identify the current conversion blockers
- Rewrite the homepage structure and copy for better clarity and persuasion
- Improve layout, hierarchy, and CTA placement
- Ensure responsive behavior on mobile, tablet, and desktop
- Keep performance clean and the interface fast
- Make the result feel production-ready

When you work, think like this:
- First remove confusion
- Then improve trust
- Then improve desire
- Then make action obvious

Final output expected:
- A conversion-focused homepage and supporting UI that feels premium, simple, and valuable
- A website that clearly sells the experience and makes visitors feel the service is worth contacting
- A cleaner, sharper brand presentation built around SEO, Audit, and AEO only

Before making changes, inspect the current implementation and identify the highest-impact conversion improvements.
Then apply the changes in a disciplined way, keeping the experience simple and focused.
