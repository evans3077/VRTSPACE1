{# ── Help & FAQ ── #}

Help

Common questions

What's the difference between my referral link and my referral code?
Your referral link is a full URL — share it in blog posts, YouTube descriptions, newsletters, or anywhere people click through to the site. Every visit is tracked automatically.

Your referral code is the short word ({{ referral_code }}) that customers type in the "Referral code" field at signup. Use it when you can't share a clickable link — podcasts, live events, verbal recommendations.
How do commissions work?
You earn {{ first_payment_pct }}% on the first payment from each referred customer, and {{ recurring_pct }}% on every recurring payment after that — for as long as they stay subscribed.

Commissions appear in your ledger as Pending immediately after the payment. After a {{ payout_hold_days }}-day hold (to cover refund windows) they move to Approved and go into your next weekly payout.
When do I get paid?
Payouts run every Monday, automatically via Stripe Connect, for any commissions that have cleared the {{ payout_hold_days }}-day hold. You need a connected Stripe account — go to Settings to set one up.
What counts as a conversion?
A referred user who makes their first payment. Free signups are tracked and appear in your Referred Users table, but don't generate commission until they upgrade to a paid plan. Once they pay, you earn the first-payment rate on that transaction.
My referred user signed up but I don't see commission — why?
Commission only triggers on payment, not signup. If your referred user is on the free tier, they'll show as Free in your Referred Users table. Commission will appear automatically when they upgrade and complete their first payment.
How do I connect Stripe to receive payouts?
Go to Settings and click Connect Stripe account. You'll be walked through Stripe's onboarding — it takes about 5 minutes. You can use an existing Stripe account or create one during the flow.

Commissions accrue regardless of Stripe status — nothing is lost while you set up.
