# Current Public Surface

## Key Files Reviewed

- `templates/base.html`
- `templates/core/home.html`
- `apps/core/views.py`
- `apps/core/site_content.py`

## Working Strengths

- Strong existing audit CTA and form foundation
- Clear existence of Audit, SEO, and AEO service blocks
- Good amount of trust, case-study, and FAQ material
- Responsive shell already exists

## Main Conversion Blockers

### Navigation exposes too much

The public header currently surfaces deeper modules and technical labels that belong later in the journey, especially content and project-control language.

### Homepage mixes multiple stories

The homepage currently mixes:

- public product entry
- platform architecture
- proprietary method exposition
- custom-work scoping
- premium technical visuals

That makes the first impression broader and noisier than the `prompts/new` direction.

### Language is more technical than needed

Terms such as citation score, semantic depth, forge, and command-heavy labels may feel impressive but slow comprehension for cold visitors.

### Custom work is too visible

Custom work belongs as an exception path, not as a co-equal headline journey.

## Implementation Implications

- Simplify navigation before adding new public sections.
- Rewrite the hero and supporting sections around outcome clarity.
- Keep the audit form, but frame advanced fields as optional context.
- Borrow the strongest product-story cues from the workspace rather than explaining internal architecture publicly.
